About the Blog

I’m a high schooler with a laptop and a dream. That’s it. That’s the vibe.

This blog started because I watched too much TV—specifically The Flash,and somehow got way too into forensic science. Like, how blood spatter patterns work. How fingerprint ridges are unique. How people leave a trail without even realizing it.

But somewhere along the way, I got more interested in the why than the how. Why people commit crimes. Why they choose certain victims. Why they confess—or don’t. Suddenly, I was less into chemical analysis and more into criminal minds. So now I’m diving into forensic psychology too: criminal profiling, courtroom behavior, mental state evaluations, all that messy, fascinating human brain stuff.

Is this a passion project? Technically, yes. But it’s also a chaotic glimpse into how I think (okay Plato). I’m not pretending to be an expert or writing like a textbook. I’m just trying to understand how forensics works in the real world, like both the lab evidence and the mental clues people leave behind.

Most posts are based on real cases or actual science. I dig through articles, interviews, documentaries, research papers, and the occasional Wikipedia panic spiral. Then I try to explain it to myself and post it here. That’s the whole process.

If you’re reading this: hi. Welcome to Trace of Evidence. I don’t know everything, but I’m figuring it out. Jesus that sounds like a 2000s movie ending, gross.
